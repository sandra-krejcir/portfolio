Return to projects

Why UX? Why Frontend?

Speaking of Frontend, it turned out to be a lot more difficult (and intimidating) than I expected it to be, however, that’s what kept my interest on edge with every passing day.

Not having experience with any of the coding languages before attending KEA, keeps me on the edge of my seat. Just as I think I've mastered something, a new trend/ feature catches my eye or a new idea pops up that motivates me to learn/ discover more.

The only downfall of coding is secludedness, and after working with the first client (Me-Mover), I realized I wanted to combine the communication, analytical, and coding skills - UX + Frontend.

And here I am, lead by the counter question of: And why not?
